## Diagnostic emphasis

- translate face/edge/vertex conditions into the correct prism, pyramid, or regular polyhedron
- fold nets and track overlapping vertices/edges, parallel edges, and skew edges
- separate the cutting plane from the rotation axis and identify both section shape and section measure
- connect cone-sector arc length, central angle, slant height, and base circumference
- distinguish unchanged outer faces, removed faces, and newly exposed cut or hole surfaces
- preserve volume across pouring, tilting, melting, and changing containers
- apply one-third factors and similarity square/cube laws without mixing surface area and volume
- translate “fits exactly” into common radius, diameter, height, cube side, and inscribed-solid constraints
